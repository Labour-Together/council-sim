This isn't a management problem.
It's a structural one.

Every council in England faces the same squeeze. In 2025/26, 350 out of 384 councils raised council tax by the maximum permitted — not because they wanted to, but because social care costs left them no choice.

Even at maximum council tax every year, a typical council cannot cover social care demand growth. The gap must come from visible services. The bins, the libraries, the parks — these aren't cut because of waste or incompetence. They're cut because adult social care is a national problem being solved with a local tax.
